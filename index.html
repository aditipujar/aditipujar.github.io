About Me

I am a Physics PhD student in the University of Texas at Austin, working with Prof. Jay Wadekar. My research interests lie at the intersection of gravitational physics, astrophysics, and data science with a focus on developing interpretable ML techniques for gravitational wave astronomy.

My earlier work too has involved applying data-driven and computational methods to varying physical systems, from low-dimensional chaos to Bayesian cell decision-making. I have previously worked with Prof. William Gilpin at UT Austin as a research assistant, where I focused on developing a meaningful embedding for unstable periodic orbits, the formative structure underlying strange attractors in chaotic systems.

I completed my BS and MS in Physics at the Indian Institute of Science, graduating in 2022. For my undergraduate and masters' theses, I worked on simulating an information-theoretic framework for biological cell decision-making, with Prof. Mohit Kumar Jolly (IISc) and Prof. Haralampos Hatzikirou (Khalifa University)

This page is an attempt at a brief overview of my work and interests, perhaps for it to serve as an informal CV. Unsurprisingly, it is very much a work in progress.
